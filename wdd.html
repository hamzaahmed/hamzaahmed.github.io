Web Design DeCal
Teaching web design to UC Berkeley students
The Web Design DeCal, more commonly known as “WDD”, is the most popular student-run course at UC Berkeley. The course is designed to introduce both experienced and first-time programmers to the realm of web development. I was only a student of WDD in Spring 2014, and now, I am glad to be a co-instructor for the design section. You can read more about WDD at wdd.io.

What I've Learned So Far
As the design co-instructor of WDD, my main role is to prepare lecture slides on a biweekly basis. I've learned that teaching design theories and philosophies such as the "pillars of emphasis" and skeumorphism can be extremely challenging. Its not easy to explain when to use serif vs. sans serif fonts, or how to strike the perfect balance of spacing such that it makes your website look simple, but not barren. Regardless, I try to do my best as a lecturer, and I definitely feel a lot more confident than when I first started teaching. I hope to keep improving to make sure WDD remains the best DeCal of all time.
